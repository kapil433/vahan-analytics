Policy angles (fuel transition & market)

  • FAME / state EV policies: cross states with EV share, charger density, and PCI to find adoption vs incentive intensity.
  • Registration-linked fiscal levers: road tax, scrappage, green cess — correlate with fuel mix shifts in the dashboard time series.
  • CNG corridors (PNGRB): align monthly CNG station roll-out with CNG vehicle registrations; flag states where infra leads or lags demand.
  • Air-quality / city programs: overlay (external) low-emission zones with EV+CNG penetration by city where disaggregated data exists.
  • Import / PLI exposure: OEM concentration (top makers) vs policy on local manufacturing — use OEM panels as a baseline.

Network & complexity science angles

  • State–state similarity: build graphs where edges = correlation or cosine similarity of fuel-mix vectors over time (experiment: export time series then network build in Python/NetworkX).
  • OEM bipartite network: states ↔ OEMs weighted by registrations; detect community structure (regional OEM strongholds).
  • Infrastructure coupling: bipartite or multilayer model linking states to EV chargers and CNG stations vs EV/CNG registration growth rates.
  • Diffusion / SIR-style metaphors: treat EV share growth as adoption curves; estimate parameters per state for forecasting benchmarks.
  • Contagion of policy: temporal ordering of state EV rules vs neighboring states’ adoption (requires external policy timeline data).
